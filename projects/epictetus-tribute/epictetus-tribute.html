Epictetus

My introducer to Stoicism

About him

Epictetus is a late greek Stoic philosopher, where in his early life worked as a slave to his master Epaphroditus, a Neros' secretary.

Lessons I learned

I am currently reading his book Discourses and Selected Writings, which is a great introduction of the Stoicism essence.

Here is what I have learned so far:

Book I

  • Concern and focus on what is under my control
  • Do not fear the outcomes of anything outside of my will
  • Test the veracity of my beliefs to guide my judgment with reason
  • Give myself a gap to properly judge what I control in the situation to not act emotionally

Conclusion

Stoicism is a powerful philosophy to cultivate virtue, despite external circumstances. Click here to download Epictetus' Discourses and Selected Writings.
